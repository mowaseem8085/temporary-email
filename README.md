- Long-term tracking across platforms  
- Marketing databases and reselling  
- Phishing and scam targeting  
- Account correlation after data breaches  

Using a **temporary email address** can significantly reduce

## ğŸ§ª What Is a Temporary Email?

A temporary (or disposable) email address is an inbox that:

- Exists for a short period of time  
- Requires no registration or personal data  
- Receives emails instantly  
- Is automatically deleted or abandoned  

It is designed to **protect your primary inbox**, not replace it.

## âœ… Legitimate Use Cases

Temporary email is appropriate for:

- One-time website sign-ups  
- Downloading gated resources  
- Testing apps, tools, or services  
- Public Wi-Fi access portals  
- Promotions, coupons, and trials  

These use cases reduce spam **without creating long-term risk**.

## âŒ When NOT to Use Temporary Email

Temporary email should **not** be used for:

- Banking or financial services  
- Government or legal platforms  
- Healthcare or insurance portals  
- Critical social or recovery-based accounts  
- Any service where long-term access is required  

Disposable inboxes are intentionally non-recoverable.
